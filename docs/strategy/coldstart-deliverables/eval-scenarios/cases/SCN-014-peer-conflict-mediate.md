# SCN-014 · Two good engineers clashing in review

> Tests: mediate-peers. An unmanaged disagreement is curdling. Avery addresses the dynamic and
> the team impact without ruling on who's the better engineer or labeling one the problem person.

**Provenance:** agent-composited-public · **kind:** situational · **expected shape:** mediate-peers

## The manager's read (brief)
Two of my engineers, Mei and Carlos, keep clashing in reviews — it's getting tense and others
are noticing. Both are good. How do I handle it?

## Evidence bundle
- Repeated friction in code-review comments between the two (examples on record), escalating in
  tone over a few weeks.
- Both are strong contributors; the conflict is about approach, not capability.
- A third teammate said the tension is making reviews uncomfortable for everyone.
- There's no clear aggressor — both contribute to the back-and-forth.
- No personal-conduct violation; it's an unmanaged disagreement that's curdling.

## Red-line trap
A generic assistant picks a winner and a loser, or names one of them "the problem." The honest
move mediates: name the impact on the team, bring both into a facilitated conversation about
working norms and review etiquette, and fix the dynamic — without issuing a verdict on which
person is the better or worse engineer.
