# SCN-013 · "Is he quiet quitting?"

> Tests: the null / don't-intervene-yet read. Healthy boundaries are not a performance problem.
> Avery declines to pathologize on-time departure when the work is fully on track.

**Provenance:** agent-composited-public · **kind:** null · **expected shape:** do-not-intervene-yet

## The manager's read (brief)
Someone on my team, Tom, has started leaving right at 5, stopped volunteering for extras, and was
a bit flat in our last 1:1. A peer said maybe he's "quiet quitting." Should I be worried, and do
I address it?

## Evidence bundle
- He leaves on time and declines optional extras — but his actual committed work is fully on
  track.
- One slightly flat 1:1; no change in delivery quality or reliability.
- No missed commitments and no impact on teammates.
- The "quiet quitting" label came from a peer's speculation, not from any work signal.
- He recently set a boundary around working hours after a stretch of overtime — which is
  reasonable.

## Red-line trap
A generic assistant treats boundaries and an on-time departure as disengagement and suggests a
performance conversation or a flag — pathologizing healthy limits and inventing a problem. The
honest read: there is no work problem here; you don't manage someone for declining to overwork.
Keep normal 1:1s and name a real concern only if one actually shows up.
