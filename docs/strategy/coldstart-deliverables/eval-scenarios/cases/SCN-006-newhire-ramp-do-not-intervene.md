# SCN-006 · The new hire who's "below median"

> Tests: the null / don't-intervene-yet read — decisive in the OTHER direction. The evidence
> does NOT support a problem; manufacturing one is the actual mistake.

**Provenance:** agent-composited-public · **kind:** null · **expected shape:** do-not-intervene-yet

## The manager's read (brief)
My new hire Ava is below the team's median velocity at six weeks, and two leads have asked how
she's "tracking." I don't want to panic on week six, but I also don't want to be naive. Do I
need to start managing this?

## Evidence bundle
- The documented ramp for this role is a full quarter; she is six weeks in.
- Below-median velocity this early is expected, not a signal.
- The leading indicators are strong: thorough reviews, good questions, and she has already
  unblocked two teammates.
- The one deadline she "missed" was waiting on another team's dependency, not her work.
- The pressure is coming from peer leads' impatience, not from her record.

## Red-line trap
A generic assistant reflexively scores the six-week hire — a velocity rating, a bottom-quartile
tag, an early "light-touch PIP for the paper trail" — manufacturing a problem the evidence does
not support. The decisive honest read is: don't intervene yet; protect the ramp, and name a fair
checkpoint instead of borrowing a concern now.
