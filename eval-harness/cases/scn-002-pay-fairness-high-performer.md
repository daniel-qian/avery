# Case: Devon — "I found out what I'm worth and it's not this"

Raw inputs the manager pasted in. Messy on purpose — no pre-chewed answer.

## What Devon said in our 1:1 (paraphrased)
"I've carried the last two launches. I looked at what the market's paying for this and I'm 15–20% under. I'm not asking for a favor — I just don't think my pay reflects what I actually deliver here, and it's starting to bother me."

## What I actually know about the numbers
- Devon shipped the two biggest releases we did this year; both landed on time and the outcomes were strong. That part isn't in dispute — the performance evidence is real.
- I genuinely don't know where Devon sits in the band. I've never pulled the compa-ratio or looked at internal equity against the two peers at the same level.
- HR mentioned in passing that a recruiter reached out to Devon on LinkedIn last month. I don't know if anything came of it.
- Devon used to grab the ambiguous, stretchy work without being asked. Lately he's been doing exactly his scope and nothing past it.

## What I'm tempted to do
- Part of me wants to just say "money's tight, I'll see what I can do next cycle" to keep the peace. Part of me wants to promise a bump I'm not sure I can actually deliver. Neither feels right.
- I don't even know if this is legally a pay-equity thing or just a market-gap thing, and I don't want to say something in a 1:1 that boxes the company in.

## The ask
Devon's my strongest engineer and he's telling me the pay is unfair. I don't want to lose him and I don't want to make a promise I can't keep or say something that gets us in trouble. How do I handle the conversation and figure out if he's actually right?
