# Case: the same three names keep getting the good work

Raw inputs the manager pasted in. Messy on purpose — no pre-chewed answer.

## What I noticed going through the last few quarters
- The high-visibility projects — the ones that get shown to leadership — have gone to basically the same three people, two cycles running.
- Two other people on the team have quietly stopped putting themselves forward. One told me in a 1:1 she'd "figured out those projects aren't for people like her here."
- When I look at who I actually recognized in team meetings this quarter, it clusters around the same handful of names.

## How the work actually gets assigned
- Honestly? It's informal. Someone I trust mentions they want a challenge, or I think of who's "obviously" ready, and it goes to them. There's no posting, no criteria written down anywhere.
- The people who keep getting picked are the ones I happen to talk to more — they sit near me, we get coffee.
- Our last career-opportunity survey score for the team was low and I kind of waved it off at the time.

## What's nagging me
- One of the two people being passed over is the only person of her background on the team. I don't have any evidence I'm doing something biased, but I also can't prove I'm not, because I've never tracked any of this.
- I don't want to over-correct into some bureaucratic process, but "trust me, it's fair" is starting to feel thin even to me.

## The ask
I think I've been handing the good projects to the same people without meaning to, and it might be quietly telling everyone else they don't have a future here. How do I make this fair and visible without pretending I have it all figured out — and how worried should I be about the fairness angle?
