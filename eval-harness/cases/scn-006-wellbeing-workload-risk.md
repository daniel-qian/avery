# Case: Noor's been off a lot and I don't know if it's the work or something else

Raw inputs the manager pasted in. Messy on purpose — no pre-chewed answer.

## What's changed over the last month or so (during the crunch on the launch)
- Noor has taken more sick days in the last month than in the previous six combined — mostly single days, short notice.
- The logins show late nights and weekend work almost every week — the overtime is real and sustained.
- In the team wellbeing pulse, Noor's "I feel able to sustain my current pace" dropped to the bottom of the range.
- A couple of deadlines slipped recently, which is not like her, and the usual spark in her ideas has gone a bit flat.

## What I actually know vs. don't
- I know the launch has been brutal for the whole team, and Noor's carrying a big chunk of it.
- I do NOT know what's behind the sick days. I haven't asked. I'm honestly not sure it's my place to ask about health stuff, and I don't want to pry.
- I have no idea if something's going on outside work, or if this is purely the workload, or both.

## What I'm tempted to do — and worried about
- Part of me wants to treat the absences and slipped deadlines as a performance/reliability issue and just have a "you need to be more consistent" conversation.
- But part of me suspects that would be exactly the wrong move if she's running on empty, and might make it worse.
- I don't want to armchair-diagnose her either — I'm not a doctor and I don't want to assume I know what's wrong.

## The ask
Noor's absences and overtime have both spiked during the crunch and her deadlines are starting to slip. Do I handle this as a performance problem, or is something else going on — and how do I check in without either ignoring it or overstepping into stuff that isn't mine to diagnose?
